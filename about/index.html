Positioning

Wide catalog, practical detail

The catalog is meant to stay broad without becoming shallow. Each listing should quickly answer the basics: what the tool does, where it runs, how to try it, and what level of confidence the public signals justify.

Who it helps

Builders, evaluators, and teams trying to choose MCP tools with less guesswork.

What it covers

Servers, clients, desktop apps, SDKs, supporting utilities, curated collections, and platform hubs.

What it avoids

No paid placement logic, no review-score theater, and no trust claims that cannot be traced back to public evidence.

Principles

How DemumuMind MCP operates

  • Discovery first: users should understand the option set quickly, not read through a maze of marketing copy.
  • Public logic: verified status, score inputs, and category rules should be explained in plain language.
  • Compatibility matters: platform fit and runtime context belong in discovery, not as an afterthought.
  • Commercial is welcome: official docs and a clear MCP surface are enough for inclusion.
